Advanced Hair Techniques We Master

At SaviqHome, we pride ourselves on staying current with the latest hair techniques while honoring time-tested methods. Our stylists regularly attend workshops and training sessions to refine their skills and learn new approaches. Click here to continue reading about our specialized techniques.

Balayage and Hand-Painted Color

Balayage is a freehand painting technique that creates natural-looking, sun-kissed highlights. Unlike traditional foil highlighting, balayage allows for more creative placement and softer, more blended results. Our colorists have mastered this technique, customizing each application to complement your natural color and desired outcome. The result is dimensional color that grows out beautifully with minimal maintenance.

Precision Point Cutting

Point cutting is a texturizing technique that creates soft, natural-looking edges and removes bulk without sacrificing length. Our stylists use this method to add movement and dimension to haircuts, creating styles that are easier to manage and style at home. This technique is particularly effective for thick hair that needs weight removal or fine hair that needs texture and body.

Keratin Smoothing Systems

Our keratin treatments use advanced formulations to smooth frizz, reduce styling time, and improve hair manageability. Unlike older straightening methods, modern keratin systems work with your hair's natural texture, enhancing rather than completely altering it. Results typically last three to five months, and the treatment actually improves hair health over time.

Dimensional Highlighting Techniques

We employ various highlighting methods including foiling, cap highlighting, and combination techniques to create depth and dimension. Our approach considers your natural base color, skin tone, and maintenance preferences. Whether you want subtle enhancement or dramatic transformation, we customize the technique to achieve your goals.

Razor Cutting Methods

Razor cutting creates soft, feathered edges and is excellent for creating textured, lived-in looks. Our stylists are trained in proper razor techniques that enhance hair's natural movement without causing damage. This method is particularly effective for creating modern, effortless styles.

Color Melting and Blending

Color melting is a technique where multiple shades are seamlessly blended together, creating a gradient effect with no harsh lines. This approach results in low-maintenance color that grows out beautifully. Our colorists carefully select complementary shades that work harmoniously with your natural color.

Professional Hair Care Guidance

Maintaining beautiful hair between salon visits requires proper care and the right products. Our stylists have compiled their best advice for keeping your hair healthy, vibrant, and manageable at home. Click here to continue learning about professional hair care.

Understanding Your Hair Type

The first step in proper hair care is understanding your hair's unique characteristics. Hair can be categorized by texture (straight, wavy, curly, coily), density (thin, medium, thick), and porosity (how well it absorbs and retains moisture). Each combination requires different care approaches. During your consultation, we assess these factors to recommend the most effective care routine for your specific hair type.

Washing Frequency and Technique

Contrary to popular belief, daily washing isn't necessary for most hair types and can actually strip natural oils that keep hair healthy. Fine hair may need more frequent washing, while thick or curly hair often benefits from less frequent cleansing. When you do wash, focus shampoo on the scalp where oil accumulates, and apply conditioner from mid-length to ends where hair needs moisture most.

Heat Styling Protection

Heat styling tools can cause significant damage if used improperly. Always apply a heat protectant product before using blow dryers, flat irons, or curling tools. Keep tools moving rather than holding them in one spot, and use the lowest effective temperature setting. Air drying when possible gives your hair a break from heat exposure.

Deep Conditioning Treatments

Regular deep conditioning treatments are essential for maintaining hair health, especially if you color or heat style your hair. These intensive treatments penetrate the hair shaft to repair damage and restore moisture. We recommend professional treatments every four to six weeks, supplemented with at-home masks weekly for optimal results.

Protecting Color-Treated Hair

Color-treated hair requires special care to maintain vibrancy and prevent fading. Use sulfate-free shampoos formulated for color-treated hair, wash with cool water (hot water opens the cuticle and releases color), and minimize sun exposure which can fade color. Regular toning treatments help maintain your desired shade between coloring appointments.

Nighttime Hair Care

How you treat your hair at night affects its condition and appearance. Sleeping on silk or satin pillowcases reduces friction that causes breakage and frizz. For long hair, loose braids or buns prevent tangling. Avoid going to bed with wet hair, which is more vulnerable to damage and can lead to breakage.

Nutrition and Hair Health

Healthy hair starts from within. A balanced diet rich in proteins, vitamins, and minerals supports hair growth and strength. Biotin, vitamin E, omega-3 fatty acids, and iron are particularly important for hair health. Staying hydrated also contributes to hair moisture and overall condition.

Trimming and Maintenance

Regular trims every six to eight weeks prevent split ends from traveling up the hair shaft and causing more extensive damage. Even if you're growing your hair out, small trims maintain shape and health. Think of trims as preventive maintenance that keeps your hair looking its best.
